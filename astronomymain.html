piAstroCam development

I am attempting to develop the hardware and software of a multifunctional astronomy webcam, based around the Raspberry Pi, its camera and Python. This will (hopefully, maybe, potentially) become a range of different image analysis programs which will do things such as: detect and count sunspots, measure magnitudes of stars in an image and track variable stars. This will eventually be available on GitHub when it is semi-useful to anyone else.

As of writing this, this "suite" of programs consists of two Python file: sunSpots.py and a image taking function which I will upload later.

Astrophotography from Tokyo

Currently, this page is a small album of photographs taken from central Tokyo with my Celestron 6SE. Cloud, haze and the light pollution of one of the most populated cities on the planet make it difficult to pick out a single constellation by eye on most evenings and it is a continual surprise to me (even as a Physics teacher) how much detail can be developed with (relatively) basic equipment, a little patience and a whole lot of exposures.

I hope ultimately to write more about the software and techniques required to process astrophotographs, but these basic photos will have to do as a placeholder for now. Equipment:

  • Celestron 6SE Schmidt Cassegrain telescope
  • Nikon D5500 camera
  • Celstron colour and moon filters
  • Logicool webcam
  • Software: IRIS and PIPP

Hardly the greatest photo of Jupiter ever taken and I was disappointed with my poor camera maintenence, allowing a huge piece of dust/dirt/general mankiness to ruin the photo. I cleaned up my camera and will need to make another attempt... better seeing and longer exposure, for better still selection, will hopefully sharpen it up. Apart from combining exposures, not much has been done to enhance the image, so playing around with sharpness settings in particular may help improve things.

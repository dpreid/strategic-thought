Solar Eclipse simulation

Another fairly basic simulation, this time of solar eclipses. This is in no way an accurate simulation of the frequency at which eclipses will occur as the orbital motion of the Earth and Moon are not simulated accurately. Relative sizes of Sun, Earth and Moon are accurate, as are mean relative distances. The Moon's inclination to the ecliptic is necessarily included to demonstrate why solar eclipses do not occur each time the Moon moves between the Earth and Sun. The Milky Way skybox was created by Adam Bielecki.

The slider sets the relative speed of the simulation: we don't want to have to wait literal years to simulate an eclipse. The bottom left camera shows the phase of the Moon. The cameras on the right show the Sun from positions in the Southern hemisphere (bottom), along the ecliptic plane (centre) and from the Northern hemisphere (top). This is to give an idea of why any single solar eclipse is only full from a relatively small area of the Earth.

Diffusion Limited Aggregation simulation

I wanted to test out Unity as a possible resource in creating educational simulations for teaching physics. As a first test of its potential, I simulated Diffusion Limited Aggregation in 3D. In later iterations I may add calculations of the fractal dimension of the growth formed, but for now the interactivity is limited to changing the attractive potential of the central "seed".
